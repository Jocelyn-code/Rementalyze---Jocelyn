Warning Signs of Suicide

By The Naional Institue of Mental Health

Talking About:

  • Wanting to die
  • Great guilt or shame
  • Being a burden to others

Feeling:

  • Empty, hopeless, trapped, or having no reason to live
  • Extremely sad, more anxious, agitated, or full of rage
  • Unbearable emotional or physical pain

Changing behavior, such as:

  • Making a plan or researching ways to die
  • Withdrawing from friends, saying good bye, giving away important items, or making a will
  • Taking dangerous risks such as driving extremely fast
  • Displaying extreme mood swings
  • Eating or sleeping more or less
  • Using drugs or alcohol more often

Mindfulness

By Crystal Hoshaw

Some examples are:

  • Gratitude list
  • Walking meditation
  • Mindful Driving
  • Single-tasking
  • Mindful Eating
  • Mindful gardening

The majority of us disregard the necessity of being mindful of the present. We frequently don't pay close attention to what is always going on in our bodies and minds. Being mindful is being conscious of your surroundings and of your own actions. It's similar to looking at your life from a distance and knowing what to do at every turn. You may comprehend your thought patterns and become more conscious of what is happening in the moment by practicing mindfulness. It enables you to see certain patterns without being mentally involved. In this manner, you can detect whether you're edging closer to a mental health issue. You can better grasp your mental processes and the events that are taking place right now by practicing mindfulness. You are able to observe those patterns by removing yourself from your thinking. This will help you recognize when a mental health issue is starting to develop.
